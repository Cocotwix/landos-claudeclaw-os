# Public-First Property Intelligence

LandOS preserves the operator's raw property input, derives structured candidates, and attempts public resolution before optional providers. A locality that is uncertain or misspelled remains visible as supplied until a source confirms it.

Resolution evidence is ordered as follows: official county/state records, federal datasets, approved parcel providers, marketplace support, and optional Land Portal cross-checks. Geocoders identify a candidate location only; they do not verify a parcel.

An address-only parcel becomes confirmed only when an exact parcel page supplies its own APN and jurisdiction, or when two independent parcel-level sources agree on a parcel identifier. A requested APN that disagrees with a resolved APN is a hard conflict: intelligence, score, valuation, offer, strategy, comps, and seller outputs remain blocked.

After confirmation, the bounded independent task set covers wetlands, FEMA flood, SSURGO soil and septic screening, slope, frontage, imagery, county facts, marketplace support, and optional Land Portal. A provider failure never stops unrelated tasks. NWI, FEMA, SSURGO, terrain, GIS frontage, and imagery are desktop-screening evidence only; recorded surveys, approved onsite findings, and jurisdictional determinations take precedence.

County capabilities and browser recipes are persisted only after observed successful verification. Recipes are versioned, become stale after failures or structural changes, and never contain credentials. Public access is attempted first. CAPTCHA, payment, phone/identity verification, material terms, automation prohibitions, unavailable managed email, and unavailable credential storage stop only that provider.

Managed government accounts may be created only for free official/public-record access when truthful registration, an approved managed email identity, secure credential storage, and safe verification are available. Passwords, verification codes, tokens, and links are transient and are never stored in Deal Card data, browser recipes, source code, logs, or fixtures.

Land Portal is an optional read-only cross-check. It never starts on page load, never blocks public research, and never overwrites stronger official evidence. Marketplace sources can support a listing or market context after identity is sufficiently established; they do not verify a parcel by themselves.

## Live public adapters currently tested

The live workflow has bounded, keyless adapters for the Tennessee Comptroller public parcel layer, Beaufort County SC archival parcel and road services, USFWS NWI, FEMA NFHL, USDA Soil Data Access/SSURGO, USGS 3DEP EPQS, and Beaufort County public 2026 imagery. They are deliberately narrow, jurisdiction-specific integrations瑈ot a nationwide support claim. Public-source results are persisted against the Deal Card by resolved APN and refresh without rerunning them. Marketplace and Land Portal remain optional, independently reported sources.
