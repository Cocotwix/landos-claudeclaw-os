business blocker and next exact fix here.

property-specific work product in this file.

### 2026-07-06 - Property Resolution as acquisition gatekeeper (workflow ordering)

- Business outcome expected: Tyler pastes messy real-world lead info exactly as received; LandOS identifies the property correctly BEFORE any downstream department runs, and the Deal Card only fills with intelligence once the parcel is actually confirmed.
- Evaluated as employees: Smart Intake (front door) + Property Resolution (gatekeeper).
- Result: Materially improved. The acquisition workflow now runs in the correct order. Property Resolution is a hard gate — downstream Property Intelligence, comparables, and Market Pulse cannot run until the parcel is established by a named source, a Browser Agent LandPortal read, independent corroboration, or a geocoded street address. A lead backed only by the operator's own pasted APN + county + road name no longer triggers a full (and therefore untrustworthy) report.
- Could Tyler paste messy info? Yes — verified live on county-only, APN-only, partial road-name address, and the full multi-line Scott County lead (county + location clues + primary + alternate APN). All organized and routed without manual cleanup.
- Would LandOS identify the property? It correctly HOLDS when it cannot yet confirm the parcel (the honest, trustworthy behavior) instead of fabricating a confident result. With Tyler's authenticated LandPortal session the Browser Agent searches both APN formats and location clues to reach the parcel; headlessly it could not complete that confirmation, so it held.
- Would the Deal Card populate correctly? Now yes — a populated Deal Card means the parcel was confirmed. Gated leads sit in "Needs Parcel Verification" with a public-records research plan and the exact next action, not fake intelligence.
- Would Property Intelligence be trustworthy? The core fix: it no longer produces intelligence on an unconfirmed parcel. When the parcel is confirmed, the existing multi-provider comps (LandPortal + Zillow + Redfin + HomeHarvest + Realie, origin-labeled, sold-prioritized, actives never mislabeled) and Market Pulse run on the VERIFIED geography.
- Measurable business value: Tyler can now trust that a Deal Card with Property Intelligence reflects a real, confirmed parcel — removing the failure mode where reports were generated for the wrong/unconfirmed property. Messy leads become organized research cards with official-source plans instead of silent bad data.
- First business blocker: full offer-readiness still needs sold comps + official confirmation (unchanged), and the live Scott County parcel confirmation needs Tyler's authenticated LandPortal browser to complete the continuous pipeline end-to-end.
- Next exact task: Tyler runs the Scott County lead from the dashboard with his logged-in LandPortal session; confirm the Browser Agent reaches the parcel, identity establishes, and the pipeline continues automatically into comps/Market Pulse/Strategy/Discovery on the verified geography.
- What not to repeat: never treat echoed operator input as a confirmed parcel; a confident-looking confidence score is not identity.
